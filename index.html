About Elena

Elena Voss was born in Buenos Aires and raised between three languages and two continents. Her writing explores memory, displacement, and the small ceremonies through which people attempt to hold things together.

Her short fiction has appeared in Granta, The Paris Review, and McSweeney's. Her debut collection, The Salt Jar, was longlisted for the Goldsmiths Prize.

Parallel to her writing practice, Elena works in stone, bronze, and clay. She studied sculpture at the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris and the Royal College of Art in London. Her work has been exhibited in galleries in Buenos Aires, Barcelona, and New York.

She currently lives and works in Oaxaca, Mexico.
